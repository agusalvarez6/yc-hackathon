# Case Study — Citibank Fraud Triage

Citibank deployed Lyra AI in 2024 to modernize fraud triage across its retail card business. Before Lyra, fraud analysts in two operations centers manually reviewed roughly 18,000 alerts per day, with an average handle time of nine minutes per alert and significant variance between analysts. The bank needed a system that could pre-classify alerts, gather context from disparate systems, and let analysts focus on judgment-heavy cases without sacrificing FFIEC examiner traceability. Priya Patel led the engagement on the Lyra side.

Lyra built a fraud triage copilot that ingests alert payloads from the bank's case management platform, retrieves customer history, transaction patterns, and prior dispositions, and proposes a recommended action with a written rationale and citations. Every recommendation is logged with the underlying evidence so an examiner can replay the decision months later.

After a six-month rollout, average handle time dropped 47 percent, false-positive rework dropped 31 percent, and the bank's QA team reported higher consistency across analysts. The deployment passed the bank's annual FFIEC model risk review with no findings, and Citibank has since extended the contract to cover wire fraud and money-movement reviews.
