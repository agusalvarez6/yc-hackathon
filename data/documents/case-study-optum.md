# Case Study — Optum Claims Automation

Optum partnered with Lyra AI in 2024 to automate first-pass review of medical claims for a regional payer subsidiary. The team handled approximately 2.4 million claims per month, with adjudication delays driven largely by missing documentation, ambiguous codes, and provider-side data entry errors. Optum needed a system that could read claim attachments, cross-reference policy and coverage data, and either auto-resolve straightforward cases or hand off enriched packages to human adjusters. Priya Patel led the engagement on the Lyra side.

Lyra built a claims automation copilot that ingests EDI 837 transactions, supporting clinical attachments, and member benefit data. The copilot proposes a disposition — pay, deny with reason, or escalate — with a written rationale citing the underlying coverage rules and clinical evidence. Every disposition is captured with full lineage for audit and appeals defense.

Within nine months of go-live, Optum saw 38 percent reduction in average claim cycle time, 22 percent reduction in adjuster-touched volume, and measurable improvement in first-pass yield on documentation requests. The deployment is HITRUST CSF certified and meets HIPAA and state-level privacy requirements; the contract has since expanded to cover prior authorization workflows.
